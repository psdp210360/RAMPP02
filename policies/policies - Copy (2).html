📘 RAM Enterprise Policy Framework

This document defines detailed governance policies across all RAM value streams. Each policy includes objectives, scope, ownership, constraints, and control requirements aligned with international best practices (IFRS, ISO 31000, ISO 55000, PMI, ITIL).

L0-1 Strategy & Portfolio Management

Corporate Strategy Policy

Owner: CIO | Standards: ISO 55000, Balanced Scorecard
Objective:
  • Define long-term strategic direction aligned with shareholder value
  • Ensure portfolio aligns with macroeconomic and sector trends
Scope:
  • All asset classes and geographic regions
Key Controls:
  • Annual strategy review cycle
  • Board approval required for strategic shifts
Constraints:
  • Capital availability
  • Regulatory restrictions
Notes:
  • Strategy must be supported by data-driven market analysis

Portfolio Governance Policy

Owner: CIO | Standards: Modern Portfolio Theory
Objective:
  • Optimize asset allocation and diversification
Controls:
  • Asset concentration thresholds
  • Risk-adjusted performance metrics
Constraints:
  • Market liquidity
  • Asset lifecycle stage
Notes:
  • Segmentation must be updated quarterly
L0-2 Investment & Capital Projects

Investment Policy

Owner: Investment Committee | Standards: IFRS, NPV/IRR Models
Objective:
  • Ensure all investments meet financial and strategic criteria
Controls:
  • Mandatory business case approval
  • Minimum IRR threshold
Constraints:
  • Funding availability
  • Market volatility
Notes:
  • All investments must undergo due diligence

CAPEX Policy

Owner: CFO / COO | Standards: PMI PMBOK
Objective:
  • Control capital expenditure lifecycle
Controls:
  • Stage-gate approvals
  • Budget variance tracking
Constraints:
  • Cost escalation risk
  • Project delays
L0-3 Commercial Management

Leasing Policy

Owner: Head of Commercial | Standards: IFRS 16
Objective:
  • Maximize occupancy and rental income
Controls:
  • Standard lease templates
  • Approval limits for discounts
Constraints:
  • Market demand
  • Tenant credit risk

Pricing Policy

Owner: Commercial Team
Objective:
  • Optimize revenue while maintaining competitiveness
Controls:
  • Benchmark pricing analysis
  • Approval matrix for incentives

Maintenance Policy

Constraints:

HSE Policy
